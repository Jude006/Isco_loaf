Our Story

Isco Loaf started as a small family bakery in 1985. Over the years, we have grown into a beloved local establishment known for our commitment to quality and tradition. Our passion for baking is rooted in our heritage, and we continue to use time-honored techniques to create our breads. Each loaf is a testament to our dedication and love for the craft.

The Art of Bread Making at Isco Loaf

Step 1: Selecting the Finest Ingredients

At Isco Loaf, quality begins with the selection of ingredients. We source our flour from local, organic farms and use only the best natural ingredients, ensuring that each loaf is not only delicious but also nutritious.

  • Organic whole wheat flour
  • Filtered water
  • Sea salt
  • Natural yeast
  • Optional: seeds, nuts, dried fruits

Step 2: Mixing and Kneading

The ingredients are carefully measured and mixed to form the dough. Kneading is an essential part of the process, developing the gluten structure that gives our bread its characteristic texture and chew.

  • Mix flour, water, and yeast until combined
  • Knead the dough until smooth and elastic
  • Let the dough rest for the initial rise

Step 3: Fermentation

Fermentation is a crucial step where the dough develops its flavor and texture. We use a slow fermentation process, allowing the natural yeast to work its magic and create a rich, complex taste.

  • Let the dough rise slowly at a controlled temperature
  • Monitor the dough's progress carefully
  • Deflate and fold the dough to redistribute the yeast

Step 4: Shaping the Loaves

Once the dough has properly fermented, it is carefully shaped by hand. This step ensures that each loaf has the perfect structure and will rise beautifully in the oven.

  • Divide the dough into individual portions
  • Shape each portion into a loaf or desired shape
  • Allow the shaped loaves to rise again

Step 5: Baking to Perfection

The final step is baking. Our bread is baked in traditional stone ovens, which provide the perfect heat distribution for a crisp, golden crust and a soft, airy interior.

  • Preheat the oven to the desired temperature
  • Place the loaves in the oven and monitor carefully
  • Bake until the crust is golden and the interior is fully cooked
  • Allow the bread to cool on racks before slicing

Step 6: Enjoying the Fruits of Our Labor

After the bread has cooled, it's ready to be enjoyed. Each loaf is a testament to our dedication and craftsmanship, bringing a little slice of joy to your table.

  • Cut slices and serve with your favorite toppings
  • Share with family and friends
  • Store any leftovers properly to maintain freshness
